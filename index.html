Past and Current Research

From Utility Scale PV installations to embedded cybersecurity, the S.M.A.R.T. Lab at Rowan (previously known as the Center for Sustainable Design) has seen many generations of students and projects. As part of the Junior and Senior Engineering Curriculum at Rowan, many of the following projects have been part of the Clinic sequence.

ISO/IEC/IEEE P21451-1 SDK

We are currently producing an SDK which can be used to develop devices which comply with the IEEE P21451-1 Standard for Smart Transducer Networks.

  • GitHub

ISO/IEC/IEEE P21451 Proof of Concept

Along with the SDK, there has been plenty of work on a proof of concept implementation, utilizing Android Applications, Raspberry Pi's, and MSP430's. Along with the code, open-source hardware such as sensor modules are being created.

  • Software
  • Hardware

Embedded Cybersecurtiy

As part of a joint reasearch project with a Government Contractor, students performed research into the basics of Cybersecurity, focusing on embedded platforms utilizing ARM core processors.

IEEE SAS 2017

Rowan University will be hosting the IEEE Sensors Applications Symposium Conference on March 13-15, 2017. SAS provides a unique opportunity for researchers and developers to share progress in sensor technologies, methods, applications, standardization, and commercialization. A mix of plenary, poster, demonstration, plug-fests, and other networking activities will be available to showcase the work being done in many areas.
